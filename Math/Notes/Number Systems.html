Introduction
Before we start learning more about irrational numbers, Lets revise the Number Systems we learnt about in earlier classes.
Natural Numbers :
The numbers that are used for counting are called Natural Numbers. Natural Numbers are denoted by 'N'. Natural Numbers include the numbers starting from 1 to Infinite. i.e {1, 2, 3, 4, 5....}.
Whole Numbers :
The set ofall Natural Numbers including zero are called Whole Numbers. Natural Numbers are denoted by 'W'. Whole Numbers include the numbers starting from 0 to Infinite. i.e {0, 1, 2, 3, 4....}.
Integers :
It is a set of numbers consisting of zero, positive numbers and negative numbers. Integers are denoted by the letter 'Z'. They include the numbers from 0 to -Infinity and 0 to +Infinity. i.e {0, 1, 2, 3, 4....} and {0, -1, -2, -3, -4...}
Note : Positive Integers include {0, 1, 2, 3, 4....} and Negative Integers include {0, -1, -2, -3, -4...}
Note : 0 is neither positive nor negative.
Rational Numbers :
The Numbers that can be Expressed in the form of p/q where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0 are called Rational numbers.
Real Numbers :
The Set consisting of all Natural Numbers, Whole numbers, Integers and Rational Numbers is called Real Numbers
Note :
N ⊂ W ⊂ Z ⊂ Q
⊆ Real Numbers

Here, N = Natural numbers, W = Whole Numbers, Z = Integers, Q = Rational Numbers
